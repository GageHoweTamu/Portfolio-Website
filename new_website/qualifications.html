One pertinent example of leadership and problem solving is my "masks for poverty" project, which I completed in June of 2021. The original problem statement was that my hometown of Sugar Land, TX had many people in poverty who couldn't get access to reusable, quality face masks, so I planned to create as many as possible within my deadline. The barrier I encountered was that I couldn't work in-person with the volunteers due to Covid-19. The way I addressed this was to assign specific jobs to each volunteer and hold weekly Zoom meetings. I also planned time to sanitize the 840 masks before delivery to the poverty center. The process felt stilted and clunky due to the remote communication, but I enjoyed the outcome and the satisfaction of knowing I made a solid difference.
